ILLINOIS TECH ELEVATE TERMS AND CONDITIONS

EFFECTIVE DATE: SEPTEMBER 15, 2025

1. ELEVATE DESCRIPTION

INTRODUCTION

Elevate is at the core of Illinois Tech's founding purpose and mission: Illinois Tech was founded in 1890 as an engine of opportunity so that children of meatpackers and machinists in Chicago could receive an excellent education and become engineers, architects, physicists, and more. Learning by doing things has always been a vital part of that mission: It's not only through books and lectures that students learn, but also through co-curricular hands-on experiences and practical applications. In today's world driven by globalization and dominated by multinational corporations, career readiness is critical to expand accessibility in modern economy.

PURPOSE

Elevate is designed to support students' academic and career growth using a data model with extensive analysis of student success variables and more than a decade of assessment of the student journey. The goal with Elevate is to help students gain experiences that will enhance their resumes throughout their college careers, and to provide skills fostering career-readiness for success in an evolving world.

ELEVATE

Illinois Tech's Elevate is designed to enhance your Illinois Tech experience and to support you throughout your learner lifecycle at Illinois Tech, starting from pre-enrollment, extending through post-enrollment and, if applicable, beyond post-graduation. Elevate combines hands-on experiential opportunities with a customized experiential learning and professional development plan (the "Elevate My Future Plan") developed in coordination with a team of career and alumni advisers, enhancing a graduate's career readiness and life skills.

CORE COMPONENTS OF ELEVATE:

  • Access to Elevate Platform: Access to the Elevate platform will be provided to students who acknowledge the requirements of the Elevate experience. Illinois Tech's Careers & Elevate Hub provides curated co-curricular and extracurricular activities and opportunities that encourage registered students to participate and advance their personal and professional development.
  • Elevate My Future Plan: Students participating in Elevate will work with advisers, success coaches, and peer mentors during your first year to create a personalized plan, mapping out a co-curricular activity and career pathway through the Elevate Platform, and an academic plan through Smart Plan.
  • Elevate Experiences: Elevate combines hands-on experiential opportunities, including short courses in general business topics, courses that introduce twenty-first century skills, student leadership opportunities, on-campus employment opportunities, competitions, study away virtual internships, internships, co-operative education and research with customized learning/experience plans.

If a student successfully completes all Elevate requirements, and an employment or graduate school offer is not achieved within six months of graduation, subject to additional factors as determined by Illinois Tech, the student may be eligible to enroll tuition-free in select graduate-level courses at Illinois Tech, up to a maximum of 9 credit hours, or by participating cost-free in relevant up-skilling programs or digital apprenticeships at Illinois Tech for the duration of one academic term. This is only available to students within one year of their graduation date.

2. ELIGIBILITY AND PARTICIPATION REQUIREMENTS

ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS FOR INITIAL ELEVATE PARTICIPATION:

  • Acknowledge the requirements of the Elevate experience. Only eligible students who execute the Elevate Acknowledgement in year one of their Illinois Tech enrollment may participate.
  • Actively participate in Elevate Orientation.
  • Regularly review, look for new opportunities, participate and accomplish the tasks outlined in the Elevate platform.
  • Opt-in and track participation, progress through the plan by completing required activities and earn Elevate experience badges for active participation or completion as applicable.
  • Meet with each member of Student Success team at least once a year for academic, financial and career plan reviews.
  • Complete a financial literacy workshop and create a financial plan to support your degree plan.
  • Remain in good financial standing throughout the degree program at Illinois Tech.
  • Maintain good academic standing and a 3.0 GPA and above throughout undergraduate degree program.
  • Complete all minimum expected requirements, and two optional experiences required during each year.
  • Graduate with a bachelor's degree within eight to 10 (for certain programs) semesters of enrollment.
  • Be in good financial standing with the university at the time of graduation.
  • Be legally eligible for employment in the United States upon graduation.
  • Be willing to relocate to a new market location, if necessary for employment, upon graduation.
  • Complete a pre-professional co-op and internship prep course.

YEAR-BY-YEAR GENERAL REQUIREMENTS TO MAINTAIN ELEVATE PARTICIPATION:

📗YEAR 1 - EXPOSURE

  • 1:1 check-in with career coach, cohort manager, or success coach and initiate co-curricular plan
  • Meet with Career Services to design, review, and complete 1st collegiate resume
  • Successfully complete an Elevate experience(s) that meets the following minimum expected requirements:
    • Aligns with field of study and/or future career path of interest
    • At least 3 hours of engagement
    • Proof of attendance is required
    • Synchronous (live in real-time, not recorded)
    • Co-curricular (not a part of a course requirement)
  • Apply for and participate in university peer mentorship program or external mentorship program
  • Chicago Adventures - Premiere City and Cultural Experiences (Community Engagement)
  • Student Organization Membership

📙YEAR 2 - ENGAGEMENT

  • Successfully complete an Elevate experience(s) that meets the following minimum expected requirements:
    • Aligns with field of study and/or future career path of interest
    • At least 10 hours of engagement
    • Must complete an activity where something tangible is produced
    • Format flexibility - can be in-person or virtual
    • Synchronous or Asynchronous (if experience is a job simulation or short course)
    • Co-curricular - must be an experience outside of the classroom
    • Requires proof of completion
  • Attend a second-year professional development workshop hosted by Career Services
  • 1:1 check-in with career coach, cohort manager, or success coach
  • Complete and optimize Handshake profile
  • Complete and optimize LinkedIn profile
  • Meet with Career Services to design, review, and complete 1st collegiate resume
  • Leadership Academy OR Approved Leadership Workshop Series

📕YEAR 3 - ACCOUNTABILITY

  • Successfully complete an Elevate experience(s) that meets the following minimum expected requirements:
    • Aligns with field of study and/or future career path of interest
    • At least 40 hours of engagement
    • Experience requires a supervisor or facilitator providing direction
    • Must be synchronous (live in real-time)
    • Co-curricular - must be an experience outside of the classroom
    • Provide proof of experience
  • Attending a professional development workshop hosted by Career Services or external conference
  • 1:1 check-in with career coach, cohort manager, or success coach

YEAR 4 - UPSKILLING

  • Successfully complete an Elevate experience(s) that meets the following minimum expected requirements:
    • Aligns with field of study and/or future career path of interest
    • At least 40+ hours of engagement
    • Can be a continuation of 3rd year experience or new upskilling experience
    • Co-curricular - must be an experience outside of the classroom
    • Provide proof of experience
  • Start and document job search or graduate school search at least six months in advance of graduation
  • 1:1 check-in with career coach, cohort manager, or success coach
  • Must apply (8) job applications per month and provide evidence – Handshake, Simplicity, LinkedIn – for external applications provide proof of completed application (i.e. email receipt)

📘YEAR 5 (IF NECESSARY) - UPSKILLING 2.0

  • Successfully complete an Elevate experience(s) that meets the following minimum expected requirements:
    • Aligns with field of study and/or future career path of interest
    • At least 40+ hours of engagement
    • Can be a continuation of 3rd year experience or new upskilling experience
    • Co-curricular - must be an experience outside of the classroom
    • Provide proof of experience
  • Start and document job search or graduate school search at least six months in advance of graduation
  • 1:1 check-in with career coach, cohort manager, or success coach
  • Must apply (8) job applications per month and provide evidence
